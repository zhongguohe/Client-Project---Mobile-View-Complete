Portfolio Support

A portfolio helps employers understand how you think, what you made, and how you solved a problem. For many roles in UX, product, research, and data, a portfolio is one of the clearest ways to demonstrate your strengths beyond a resume.

This page provides practical guidance for building a clear, professional portfolio that highlights your contributions, shows your process, and helps recruiters quickly understand your work.

Portfolio Basics: What to Include

  • A short introduction: who you are, what roles you are targeting, and what kind of work interests you.
  • 2–4 strong projects: quality matters more than quantity.
  • Clear navigation: employers should be able to find your best work in 1–2 clicks.
  • Your role and contributions: clearly explain what you did, not only what the team did.
  • Contact and links: include your email, LinkedIn, and GitHub if relevant.

How to Write a Strong Case Study

Good case studies are not long essays. They are structured explanations that help readers quickly understand the challenge, your process, your decisions, and the outcome.

Recommended Structure

  1. Problem: What challenge were you solving? Who was the user or stakeholder?
  2. Goal: What success looked like.
  3. Process: Methods you used, such as research, analysis, prototyping, or testing.
  4. Key decisions: Why you chose one direction over another.
  5. Outcome: What changed, what you delivered, and the impact.
  6. Reflection: What you learned and what you would do differently next time.

Tip: Show Evidence

Include visuals and concrete examples whenever possible, such as wireframes, diagrams, prototype screenshots, charts, interview themes, or usability findings.

Your goal is not to include everything. Your goal is to include the pieces that best help someone understand your thinking and contribution.

Portfolio Ideas by Career Path

UX / Product Design

  • User research summaries
  • Journey maps or task flows when they informed decisions
  • Wireframes and prototypes
  • Testing results and design iterations

Research

  • Research question and study design
  • Recruitment approach and interview guide
  • Themes and insights
  • Recommendations and limitations

Data / Analytics

  • Problem statement and dataset context
  • Cleaning and analysis approach
  • Visualizations and key insights
  • Deliverables and takeaways

Portfolio Checklist Before You Share

  • Your homepage clearly states your target roles.
  • Projects have short summaries so employers can scan quickly.
  • Each case study includes your role, tools, and timeline.
  • Images are readable and captions help explain what the viewer is seeing.
  • Links work and your contact information is easy to find.
  • Your strongest project is placed first or clearly highlighted.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Too much text: use headings, bullets, and images to make your story easier to scan.
  • Unclear personal contribution: clearly describe what you owned and delivered.
  • Only showing final screens: include process and iteration, not just polished results.
  • No outcomes: include results, insights, or impact whenever possible.
  • Broken navigation: test every link and every page before sharing your site.
